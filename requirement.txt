1️⃣ PyInstaller + UPX (best for compression)
	1.	Install UPX: https://upx.github.io/ and make sure upx.exe is in your PATH.
	2.	Build with PyInstaller:
	pyinstaller --noconsole --onefile --add-data "assets;assets" --upx-dir C:\path\to\upx billing_dashboard.py
		•	--upx-dir points to UPX binary location.
	•	UPX compresses the EXE, often reducing size by 40–60%.

Pros: Very simple, single EXE, compressed.
Cons: Some antivirus programs may flag heavily compressed EXEs.


2️⃣ Nuitka + UPX
	•	Nuitka can produce .exe and then you can compress with UPX:
	python -m nuitka --onefile --windows-disable-console --enable-plugin=qt-plugins --include-data-dir=assets=assets --follow-imports billing_dashboard.py
upx --best --lzma billing_dashboard.exe

	•	--best --lzma is UPX’s maximum compression mode.

Pros: Smallest, fastest EXE.
Cons: Might fail on very complex dependencies; debugging harder.
